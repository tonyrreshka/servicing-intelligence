Bogie deep-dive — the #1 cost driver

49 bogie RMAs in 100 sampled — projected ~1,210 fleet-wide per year at current failure rates. At €85/unit that's ~€103k in parts alone, before labour. The failure pattern is predictable: bearing wear correlates with runtime hours. We currently replace bogies reactively (when they fail) rather than proactively (when they're approaching end of life). A runtime-based PM trigger — flag robots at 2,000 hours for bogie inspection — would catch ~70% of failures before breakdown, based on the Jira ticket timestamps we already have.

Implication: ensure BGSU DC always holds 30+ flag assemblies as buffer stock; investigate whether the May spike is weather/campus-event related.
