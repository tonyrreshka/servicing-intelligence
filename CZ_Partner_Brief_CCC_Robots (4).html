Chassis / motor issues
Chassis wear and tear or body deformation from collision or severe weather. Sub-assembly swaps (drive motors, bogies etc) are field-repairable. Full chassis swap also possible on-site. Major multi-system structural faults escalate to T3.

Charging port
Wear item. Connector replacement or port assembly swap. Electrical test required post-repair before returning to service.
⏱ 30–60 min  Band A–B  RSE identifies components
Field scenarios
What repairs look like in practice
Flag / antenna replacement
Most frequent job. Remove damaged flag assembly from pole mount, fit replacement, confirm secure. Basic hand tools only.
⏱ Band A · 15–30 min on-site
Wheel assembly replacement
Wheel assembly swap on the 6-wheel platform (yellow-rim wheels). Full drive test on flat ground required before reactivation on public roads.
⏱ Band B · 30 min · Drive test required
Module / sensor replacement
Radar or camera module swap. Requires alignment verification with Starship diagnostics tool post-install. Follow repair manual step-by-step.
⏱ Band B · 2–4 hrs · Diagnostics tool required

Day in the life
What a typical day looks like
08:00
RSE — remote triage & ticket assignment
Starship Remote Support Engineers review all open tickets, run remote diagnostics, identify required parts, and assign pre-diagnosed jobs to the field team. Each ticket handed to you already includes: fault description, complexity band, parts list, site address, and relevant repair manual.
Starship RSE · not your task
08:30
3rd party — load van, review assigned tickets
Check your assigned ticket queue. Load van with parts specified on each ticket. Collect or rescue any bots that need to be brought back to the service hub for more complex repairs. Flag any parts shortages before leaving — never attend a site without the right parts.
Band A–B jobs
09:15
3rd party — first site visit (Band A: flag/wheel)
Arrive at store. Deactivate robot per protocol. Execute the repair as specified in the ticket (flag, wheel, or other Band A job). Run post-repair verification test. Photo, log parts used, close ticket. For anything beyond Band A, collect the robot and bring to the service hub.
Band A · 15–30 min
11:00
3rd party — service hub: component replacements
Back at the service hub with any robots collected from the field. Execute Band B repairs (camera modules, radar, electronics) following RSE instructions and repair manuals. Use the Starship diagnostics tool for sensor alignment and verification. Confirm each robot passes post-repair checks before returning to service.
Band B · 1–4 hrs · Diagnostics tool
14:30
3rd party — documentation & ticket close
Close all open tickets with repair notes, parts used, before/after photos, and verification results. All tickets must be documented within 2 hours of completing the work. Track all activity through the Starship ticketing tool — no verbal updates or undocumented repairs.
Documentation · within 2 hrs
15:30
3rd party — return repaired bots + van restock
Return repaired robots to their deployment sites and confirm they are back in service. Restock van with any parts used today — raise a parts request ticket if any item drops below minimum stock. Review tomorrow's ticket queue so you start the next day ready to go.
End of day · handover complete
